Discover Georgia: Where Europe Meets Asia

Georgia is a country located at the crossroads of Eastern Europe and Western Asia, known for its dramatic mountain landscapes, ancient history, and warm hospitality. From the charming streets of Tbilisi to the snowy peaks of the Caucasus Mountains, Georgia offers a rich and unforgettable travel experience.

With a history dating back thousands of years, Georgia is one of the world‚Äôs oldest wine-producing regions. Its unique alphabet, traditional music, and deep-rooted customs make Georgian culture truly distinctive.

Culture and Traditions

Georgian culture is deeply connected to family, hospitality, and tradition. Guests are treated with great respect, and sharing meals is an important social ritual led by a toastmaster known as the Tamada.

Traditional polyphonic singing, recognized by UNESCO, and folk dances are central to Georgia‚Äôs cultural identity and celebrations.

Places to Visit

Georgia offers a wide range of attractions, including the historic capital Tbilisi, the cave city of Uplistsikhe, and the ancient monasteries of Mtskheta.

Nature lovers can explore Kazbegi National Park, hike in the Caucasus Mountains, or relax by the Black Sea coast in Batumi.

Famous Places to Eat and Dine

Georgian cuisine is hearty and flavorful, featuring dishes such as Khachapuri (cheese-filled bread), Khinkali (dumplings), and Lobio. Meals are often paired with traditional Georgian wine.

Food plays a central role in Georgian culture, with long feasts that celebrate friendship, storytelling, and togetherness.

Transportation

Transportation in Georgia is affordable and accessible. Tbilisi offers buses, metro services, and taxis, making it easy to navigate the city.

Marshrutkas (minibuses) connect cities and towns, while international flights operate through Tbilisi International Airport.
